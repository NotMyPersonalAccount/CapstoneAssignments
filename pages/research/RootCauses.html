Root Causes

Root Cause #1

Reliability & Uncertainty

Evidence

Developers are often not paid for contributions. [1]

Explantion

Developers can stop contributing at any time, leaving projects unmaintained.

Evidence

Project reputations matter. [1]

Explantion

Reputable projects are more likely to be trusted, and thus adopted.

Implications

This issue can be solved through sponsorship of FLOSS projects. Developers will get funding to work on the project and the project will be more reputable with a company behind it.

Examples: GNOME development sponsored by Red Hat

Root Cause #2

Technological Vendor Lock-In

Evidence

Organizations may already be using proprietary formats. [1]

Explantion

Those formats may not be supported, or poorly supported, by open source software.

Evidence

Resources are needed to switch. [1]

Explantion

It takes financial and human resources to support the switch away from existing software.

Implications

This issue can be solved with more common usage of open standards. Therefore, formats can continue to be used both internally and externally for an organization, and less effort is required to migrate.

References

  1. Petrov, Dmitrij and Obwegeser, Nikolaus, Adoption Barriers of Open-Source Software: A Systematic Review (March 11, 2018).
